Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters. Here's exactly how we handle your data.

Data Collection

Atlas Logged does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data, location data, or any other user-generated content to our servers.

All data remains on your device, or optionally in your personal iCloud account if you enable sync. We have no servers, no databases, and no way to access your information.

Apple Services Only

Atlas Logged uses exclusively Apple's built-in frameworks and services:

  • Core Location - for location tracking (requires your permission)
  • Core Data - for local data storage on your device
  • CloudKit - for optional iCloud sync (requires iCloud to be enabled)
  • PhotoKit - for optional photo access (requires your permission)
  • UserNotifications - for optional local notifications (requires your permission)

We do not use any third-party analytics, tracking, advertising, or cloud services. Everything runs through Apple's secure, encrypted infrastructure that only you control.

Data Storage

Information like travel history and calendar entries is stored locally on your device using iOS Core Data.

There is no external backup or transmission of data to third parties. If you enable iCloud sync, your data is encrypted and synchronized exclusively through Apple's iCloud service, which we do not have access to.

All cloud features use exclusively Apple's iCloud service. We do not operate any servers or third-party cloud services.

Multi-Device Sync (Optional)

This feature is entirely optional and requires iCloud to be enabled.

If you choose to use Atlas Logged on multiple devices, device metadata is synchronized via Apple's iCloud to coordinate tracking between devices. This includes:

  • Device name (e.g., "John's iPhone")
  • Device type (iPhone, iPad, or Mac)
  • Last known location (latitude/longitude coordinates)
  • Battery level
  • Last active timestamp
  • Primary/secondary device role

This metadata helps coordinate which device actively tracks your location. Only iPhones can be primary tracking devices; iPads and Macs are always secondary devices that display data but don't actively track location.

All device metadata is synced exclusively through Apple's iCloud using encrypted CloudKit containers. This data is only accessible to your devices signed in with your Apple ID.

Photos Access (Optional)

Photo library access is entirely optional and requires your explicit permission.

If you grant photo library access, Atlas Logged can display photos from your travels alongside your location history. The app:

  • Only reads photo metadata (creation date and location information)
  • Never modifies, uploads, or shares your photos
  • Displays photos locally that match the dates and locations you visited
  • Clusters photos by location on the map for easy viewing

Photo access uses Apple's PhotoKit framework exclusively. Photos remain in your device's photo library and are never copied, transmitted, or stored elsewhere.

You can revoke photo access at any time in iOS Settings without affecting location tracking functionality.

Notifications (Optional)

Notification access is entirely optional and requires your explicit permission.

If you enable notifications, Atlas Logged can send you local notifications about your travel activity and app status. All notifications are generated locally on your device using Apple's UserNotifications framework.

We do not send push notifications from external servers. You can disable notifications at any time in iOS Settings.

Location Data

Location tracking requires your explicit permission and uses Apple's Core Location framework exclusively.

Location tracking happens entirely on your device. You control the precision level:

  • Country Level: 20-50km threshold
  • City Level: 1-20km threshold
  • Precise: 25-2000m threshold
  • Maximum: 5-200m threshold

Location data is processed locally using Apple's Core Location APIs and never leaves your device unless you manually export it or enable iCloud sync (which uses Apple's encrypted iCloud service). You can change or revoke location permissions at any time in iOS Settings.

Security

Data security depends on your individual device protection. We recommend:

  • Using strong device passwords or biometric authentication
  • Keeping your iOS software updated
  • Enabling Face ID/Touch ID for app document protection

Children's Privacy

Because all data is stored locally and we collect no information, there are no specific data collection considerations for children's privacy. The app does not distinguish between users of different ages.

Changes to This Policy

We will notify users of any significant changes to this privacy policy through app updates. Users can opt-out of any external data sharing features if they are introduced in the future.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy, contact us at: support@atlascodes.ai

Bottom Line: Your location data is yours. We can't see it, we can't access it, and we can't share it. It stays on your device or in your personal iCloud account (if you enable sync). All optional features (iCloud sync, multi-device, photos) use exclusively Apple's services and require your explicit permission. That's the way it should be.
